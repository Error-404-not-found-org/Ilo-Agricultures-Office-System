# Breeding and Reproductive Management Guide

To manage breeding successfully or program reproductive data into a management system, you need to understand three core phases: **heat detection (the trigger)**, the **AI countdown (the timing)**, and **gestation (the countdown to calving)**.

While dairy and beef cattle share almost identical biological clocks, the swamp buffalo—locally known as the **Carabao**—has a distinct reproductive cycle that presents unique management challenges.

---

## 1. The General Reproductive Timeline

| Animal Type | Estrous Cycle (Interval between heats) | Duration of Standing Heat | Best Time for AI (Conventional Semen) | Gestation Period (Pregnancy length) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Cattle (Dairy & Beef)** | **21 Days** *(Range: 18–24 days)* | **12 to 18 Hours** *(Can be as short as 6–8 hrs)* | **12 to 16 Hours** after the first signs of standing heat | **283 Days** *(Range: 275–290 days)* |
| **Carabao (Water Buffalo)** | **21 Days** *(Range: 13–28 days)* | **18 Hours** *(Highly variable: 5–36 hrs)* | **12 to 18 Hours** after onset *(Best results if bred close to ovulation)* | **320 to 325 Days** *(River buffaloes are slightly shorter)* |

## 2. The Insemination Countdown (The AM-PM Rule)

For both cattle and carabaos, ovulation occurs *after* the animal goes out of heat. Because sperm cells need 6 to 8 hours inside the female reproductive tract to mature (a process called *capacitation*), **timing is everything.**

### Cattle Countdown

Cattle follow the traditional **AM-PM Rule** for maximum conception rates:

* **Seen in Standing Heat in the AM:** Inseminate that same **PM** (afternoon).
* **Seen in Standing Heat in the PM:** Inseminate the next **AM** (morning).

> ⚠️ **Note on Sexed Semen:** If using sexed semen in cattle, delay the countdown. Sexed semen has a shorter lifespan, so it is recommended to inseminate **18 to 24 hours** after the start of standing heat.

### Carabao Countdown (The "Silent Heat" Challenge)

Carabaos ovulate roughly **34 hours after the onset of heat** (or 15 hours after heat ends). While the timeline matches cattle, carabaos are notorious for **silent heat**—they rarely show obvious behavioral signs like standing to be mounted.

* **The Blueprint:** Heat incidence peaks at night (6:00 PM to midnight). Technicians look for secondary cues: a clear, stringy vaginal mucus discharge, restlessness, and frequent urination.
* **AI Protocol:** Because behavioral signs are weak, many farms use **Timed AI (TAI)** protocols using hormones (like GPG or Ovsynch) to bypass heat detection entirely and trigger ovulation on a strict countdown timer.

## 3. Fixed-Time Artificial Insemination (FTAI) Protocols

Hormonal synchronization protocols are widely used in buffalo management to achieve satisfactory pregnancy rates without the need for visual heat detection.

```
[OVSYNCH PROTOCOL TIMER]
  Day 0: GnRH Injection (Triggers ovulation of dominant follicle & starts new wave)
    │
    ▼ (Wait 7 Days)
  Day 7: PGF2α Injection (Causes luteolysis/regression of the corpus luteum)
    │
    ▼ (Wait 48 Hours / 2 Days)
  Day 9: GnRH Injection (Triggers LH surge & synchronizes ovulation)
    │
    ▼ (Wait 16 Hours)
  FTAI (Fixed-Time Artificial Insemination)
```

### 3.1 The Standard Ovsynch Protocol
Developed to synchronize ovulation, Ovsynch uses three injections on a strict schedule:
1. **Day 0:** Inject **GnRH** (Gonadotropin-Releasing Hormone) to induce ovulation of any existing dominant follicle and initiate a new follicular wave.
2. **Day 7:** Inject **PGF2α** (Prostaglandin F2-alpha) to regress any functional corpus luteum (luteolysis).
3. **Day 9 (48 hours post-PGF):** Inject a second dose of **GnRH** to induce a luteinizing hormone (LH) surge and synchronize ovulation.
4. **FTAI Window:** Perform Fixed-Time AI **16 hours** (range: 16–20 hours) after the second GnRH injection.

### 3.2 The CIDR-Synch Protocol (Progesterone-Based)
For anestrous buffaloes or during the low-breeding season, standard Ovsynch may fail because animals lack active cyclic ovaries. Incorporating a progesterone-releasing device improves ovulation success:
1. **Day 0:** Insert a **CIDR** (Controlled Internal Drug Release) device intravaginally and administer the first **GnRH** injection.
2. **Day 7:** Remove the **CIDR** and administer a **PGF2α** injection.
3. **Day 9 (48 hours post-removal):** Administer the second **GnRH** injection.
4. **FTAI Window:** Perform AI **16 to 18 hours** after the GnRH injection.

> 📊 **FTAI Conception Rates:** In swamp buffaloes, timed AI protocols typically yield **30% to 50%** conception rates depending on the season, nutrition, and management quality.

## 4. Seasonal Constraints and Reproductive Physiology

### 4.1 Seasonal Anestrus
Swamp buffaloes are highly sensitive to environmental factors. During hot, dry months, they experience **seasonal anestrus**—a period of sexual inactivity characterized by inactive ovaries.
* **Mechanism:** Heat stress reduces hormone levels (specifically LH secretion), preventing dominant follicles from maturing and ovulating.
* **Best Breeding Season:** Fertility and cycling improve during cooler months (rainy/winter seasons) when temperature-humidity indexes (THI) decline.

### 4.2 Sperm Capacitation and Maturation
Once semen is deposited:
* **Capacitation Period:** Buffalo sperm requires **6 to 8 hours** of exposure to the female uterine and cervical environment to undergo capacitation (stripping of glycoprotein coats and membrane changes) before they are capable of fertilizing the egg.
* **Timing Sensitivity:** Inseminating too late (post-ovulation) or too early (exceeding the fertile life of sperm, which is about 24 hours) drastically reduces conception rates.

## 5. Post-Calving Recovery and Uterine Involution

To maintain an efficient calving interval, the animal's uterus must recover quickly from pregnancy and return to normal size (uterine involution).

```
                      [POST-CALVING PROTOCOL]
                                 │
           ┌─────────────────────┴─────────────────────┐
           ▼                                           ▼
   [CATTLE PATHWAY]                            [CARABAO PATHWAY]
• Involution: ~40 Days                      • Involution: ~60 Days
• Target Breeding: <82 Days                 • Target Breeding: 60 - 90 Days
• Calving Interval: 12 Months               • Calving Interval: 14 - 15 Months
           │                                           │
           └─────────────────────┬─────────────────────┘
                                 ▼
                     [UTERINE FLUSHING PROTOCOL]
                     • 4% Povidone-Iodine solution
                     • Administered within 48h postpartum
                     • Speeds involution to ~30 Days
```

### 5.1 Involution Timelines
* **Cattle:** Natural uterine involution takes **40 days**. The target breeding window is **82 days post-calving** to maintain a 12-month calving interval.
* **Carabaos:** Swelling and tissue healing take longer, requiring **60 days**. The target breeding window is **60 to 90 days post-calving**, resulting in a standard **14-to-15-month** calving interval.

### 5.2 Uterine Flushing (Accelerated Recovery)
To combat subclinical endometritis (uterine inflammation) and accelerate calving readiness:
* **Protocol:** Flush the uterus with a **4% Povidone-Iodine solution** within **48 hours postpartum**.
* **Efficacy:** Cleans out bacteria and tissue debris, reducing the time required for complete uterine involution from 60 days down to **approximately 30 to 35 days**. This allows for earlier timed inseminations and shorter calving intervals.

## 6. Breed-Specific Nuances

### Cattle (Dairy vs. Beef)

* **Dairy (e.g., Holstein-Friesian, Jersey, Brown Swiss):**
  * **Gestation:** Holsteins average **280 to 282 days**. Brown Swiss tend to carry slightly longer (~288 days).
  * **Management:** High-producing dairy cows exhibit shorter, weaker standing heats due to metabolic demands burning off estrogen.

* **Beef - British & Continental (e.g., Angus, Hereford, Simmental, Limousin):**
  * **Gestation:** Averages **283 to 285 days**.
  * **Characteristics:** Reach puberty earlier (10–14 months) and express distinct behavioral heat.

* **Beef - Tropically Adapted / Zebu (e.g., Brahman, Nelore):**
  * **Gestation:** Longest among cattle, often ranging from **288 to 292 days**.
  * **Characteristics:** Reach puberty much later (16–24 months). Like carabaos, Brahmans frequently display shorter heats that occur mostly during cooler night hours.

### Carabao & Buffalo Breeds

* **Philippine Carabao (Swamp Buffalo):**
  * **Gestation:** **320 to 325 days** (roughly 10.5 months).
  * **Traits:** Used heavily for draft work and meat. Slower reproductive rate if nutrition is poor.

* **Murrah / Nili-Ravi (River Buffalo):**
  * **Gestation:** **310 to 318 days** (significantly shorter than the native swamp type).
  * **Traits:** Primary dairy buffalo breeds. They exhibit higher milk production and slightly cleaner reproductive cycle cues than pure carabaos.

* **Crossbreds (Murrah x Carabao Grades):**
  * **Gestation:** Splits the difference, usually averaging **315 to 320 days**.
